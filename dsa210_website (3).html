The Initial Hypothesis Hypothesis Rejected

"We started this project with a traditional football assumption:

A higher Domesticity Index — more players competing in their local home leagues — creates better tactical cohesion and leads to greater national team success."

After analysing 160 national team observations spanning 1986 to 2024, the data told a very different story. The Pearson correlation between the Domesticity Index and Success Score was nearly zero — and the T-test returned a p-value well above 0.05. The hypothesis collapsed entirely.

Results

Key findings

Three major discoveries emerged from statistical testing and machine learning applied to 160 national team observations.

01
Domesticity hypothesis rejected
Pearson r ≈ −0.048, T-test p-value > 0.05. Statistically confirms that having a high concentration of local players is no longer a prerequisite for international success in the modern era.
02
FIFA Rank & Market Value dominate
FIFA Rank (r = −0.27) and Market Value (r = 0.21) consistently outperform all demographic variables. Financial strength and pre-tournament ranking are the most reliable predictors.
03
Globalization trend confirmed
Top 5 League Ratio shows r = 0.19. Since the 1990s, elite-league representation in successful squads has increased by ~12.63%. Exporting talent beats domestic retention.

Approach

Methodology

A four-stage data science pipeline from raw data collection through to machine learning evaluation.

1

Data collection

Hybrid pipeline combining Kaggle datasets (tournament results), BeautifulSoup scraping of Transfermarkt archives (squad demographics, market values), and manually curated UCL/UEL trophy data — 160 observations across teams from 1986 to 2024.

2

Exploratory data analysis

Scatter plots, correlation heatmaps, and time-series line plots to understand variable distributions and reveal the globalization trend. Success Scores normalized by tournament stage (Winner = 100, Runner-up = 80, etc.).

3

Hypothesis testing

Pearson Correlation measured the linear relationship between Domesticity Index and Success Score. Independent T-Tests compared high-domesticity vs. low-domesticity squads. Significance threshold: p < 0.05.

4

Machine learning

Regression models (Linear, KNN, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, SVR, Lasso, Ridge) and classification tasks (Low/Mid/High success bins). 5-Fold Cross-Validation throughout. Best k for KNN selected via CV grid search (k=3 to 15).

Discussion

Limitations & future work

Constraints of the current study and directions for future investigation.

League quality not weighted

The Domesticity Index treats all domestic leagues equally — UEFA coefficients could add nuance between strong and weak domestic environments.

Historical financial data

Market values from the late 1980s–early 1990s were estimated from historical records, introducing a minor margin of error.

Unquantified external factors

Coaching philosophy, key player injuries, and squad psychology were excluded — these qualitative factors can significantly influence outcomes.

Weighted Domesticity Index

Incorporate league strength via UEFA/FIFA coefficients to distinguish between high-quality and lower-quality domestic environments.

Player fatigue analysis

Integrate total minutes played before the tournament to test whether high-value squads underperform due to burnout.

Broader geographical scope

Expand to AFC and CAF tournaments to determine if the globalization trend is worldwide or Europe-specific.
